Electric Longboard

Custom belt-drive build — design, wiring, and enclosure fabrication.

I designed and assembled a DIY longboard: 10S2P 36V battery, ESC setup, 1800W motor, and a custom enclosure modeled in CAD. The goal was a reliable daily cruiser to and from my school.

  • ← Back to Portfolio

Build Specs

Core components & configuration

Drive

Single drive belt system
5M gear, 270 mm belts
Compatible wheels: 83 mm / 90 mm / 97 mm

Motor

1800 W brushless hub motor
Optimized for high torque & cruising stability

Battery & Electronics

36 V 25 Ah 21700 Lithium Pack (10S2P)
Integrated BMS + charger
Single drive ESC + remote controller

Process

From CAD to first ride

Design & CAD

Modeled the enclosure to fit the curvature of the longboard. Designed using a circular sector geometry to optimize aerodynamics. Added raised walls to snug battery in place.

Mounting Motor

Installed the 1800W hub motor. Drilled holes in the wheel to safely connect the wheel with the mount. Connected the mount with the motor using a single drive belt system.

Features

Key capabilities that delivers smoother and more enjoyable ride

Top Speed

Reaches speeds up to 17 km/h, delivering smooth acceleration and control.

Rechargeable

3D designed a charging hole to convinently charge the board without having to replacing the battery.

Remote Control

Control acceleration and braking with a wireless remote for a responsive ride.

Battery Indicator

Real-time battery level display to keep track of your charge while riding.
